The late Mr. Ruben H. Santos is remembered for his significant contributions to Makati High School. He played a pivotal role in the school's growth, from overseeing 361 students and 14 teachers to managing over 6,000 students and nearly 300 teachers. Mr. Santos served as the principal of MHS for 23 years, from 1968 to 1991, demonstrating his unwavering dedication to his profession and selfless service to his students and colleagues.
Despite the immense challenges and various problems he encountered, Mr. Santos remained steadfast and determined in fulfilling his mission. His responsibilities as a school principal were monumental, but he faced them with strength and a resolute commitment to providing the best education for MHS and fostering the best qualities in its students.
Throughout his endeavors, Mr. Santos embraced a guiding principle of having faith in oneself, maximizing one's talents, and seeking the guidance of the Lord. Prior to his tenure at MHS, he taught at Makati Elementary School, where he met his wife, Mrs. Marina Tatlonghari Santos. Together, they raised four intelligent sons. He also taught at Torres High School and Pio del Pilar Educational Institute. In 1968, he became the teacher in charge of Makati High School and swiftly advanced to the position of principal in 1970.
Mr. Santos received numerous awards and plaques in recognition of his exemplary performance and leadership. Additionally, he served as the President of the Association of Secondary Schools. His impact on Makati High School and the education community is indelible, and his memory continues to be cherished.
